MISSION

The Association for Academic Women (AAW) connects professional women throughout the University of Florida community to enable personal growth, relationship building, career development and lifelong learning - and to have some fun!!

We provide valuable networking and mentoring opportunities through special programs, social events, and monthly networking luncheons; and act as an advisory group to higher administration by providing valuable information on UF women's needs and desires. Our organization fosters career and personal growth for UF women in all faculty, administrative, and professional roles by providing an interdisciplinary, university-wide platform and encouraging the building and strengthening of relationships among our members on and off campus.

HISTORY

Since its early beginnings, the Association for Academic Women (AAW) has been known for its rich history. What began as the Committee on the Status of Women, identifying, advocating for and increasing awareness of the needed changes in the University environment in order to enhance opportunities for women, evolved to become the Association for Academic Women (AAW). Today, the organization is dedicated to providing a forum for topics of interest to women, serving as an advisory group to the University Administration and supporting the professional development of faculty and administrative staff campus-wide.

AWARDS

Madelyn Lockhart Dissertation Fellowship Award

The Association for Academic Women (AAW) at the University of Florida established the Madelyn Lockhart Dissertation Fellowship in 1996 to honor an outstanding graduate student. One fellowship in an amount up to $2,000 will be awarded to assist in the dissertation phase of the doctoral degree. The recipient must be enrolled in dissertation credits for at least one semester (for example summer 2016 or fall 2016 and/or spring, 2017) after s/he is selected to receive the fellowship.
